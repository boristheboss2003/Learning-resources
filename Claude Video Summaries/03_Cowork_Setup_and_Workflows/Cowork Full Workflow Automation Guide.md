**Cowork vs Chat vs Code --- Choose the Right Mode**

  ---------- ------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------
  **Mode**   **Best For**                                                        **When NOT to Use**
  Chat       Quick questions, single-shot answers                                Anything touching files or recurring work
  Code       Developers writing scripts or building products                     Non-coders without technical skills
  Cowork     Multi-step tasks, file work, repetitive work, file+research combo   Quick one-off questions
  ---------- ------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------

The four situations where Cowork is the right choice:

1.  Complex tasks you want handled start to finish.

2.  Anything that touches your local files directly.

3.  Repetitive work you do every week.

4.  Anything combining file work with online research.

**Setup --- The Most Important Section**

The host calls setup non-negotiable. Skip it and outputs feel generic. Do it right and Cowork behaves like a trained employee.

**Step 1 --- Dedicated Working Folder**

Don\'t point Claude at your full Documents folder. Create a dedicated folder. Host\'s name: Cowork-Station.

Three subfolders inside:

  --------------- -------------------------------------
  **Subfolder**   **Purpose**
  context/        What Claude needs to know about you
  projects/       Active work
  output/         Where finished deliverables land
  --------------- -------------------------------------

**Step 2 --- Three Context Files**

Inside the context folder, three markdown files that Claude reads automatically at the start of every session:

**about-me.md**

-   Your role.

-   Your business.

-   Your audience.

-   What you care about.

-   Two paragraphs is enough.

**brand-voice.md**

-   Your tone.

-   Your style.

-   Examples of how you actually write.

-   Words you use.

-   Words you never use.

**working-preferences.md**

-   How you want documents structured.

-   How Claude should handle ambiguity (make a call vs ask first).

**Step 3 --- Global Instructions**

Settings → Cowork → Global Instructions. Set defaults: tone, role, output format. The standing brief for every session. Five minutes of setup, weeks of friction removed.

**Real Demonstration: Six Client Meeting Transcripts**

The host\'s live demo: process six raw meeting transcripts (discovery calls, strategy sessions, follow-ups) and produce client deliverables.

**The Prompt**

> *Process these six meeting transcripts. For each client, create: 1) An Excel project tracker with tasks, owners, deadlines, and priority levels. 2) A follow-up email under 150 words confirming next steps. Use professional but conversational tone. Save all files to the output folder.*

**What Cowork Does (in Parallel)**

-   Reads multiple transcript files simultaneously.

-   Extracts task data, owners, and deadlines from context.

-   Calculates real calendar dates from phrases like \'end of next week\'.

-   Cross-references information across transcripts.

**Output (3 Minutes Later)**

-   Excel files with proper column structure.

-   Conditional formatting on priority levels.

-   Summary row at the bottom of each tracker.

-   Follow-up emails referencing specific discussion points from each call.

-   Action items confirmed with real deadlines.

-   Each email under 150 words, ready to send.

> *This is two to three hours of manual work done in three minutes.*

**YouTube Niche Research Workflow**

Real research task that normally takes 2 hours of tab-switching.

**Prompt**

> *Search YouTube for top-performing videos in the AI tools niche from the last 30 days. For each of the top 10, extract title, view count, hook style, main topic. Identify three content gaps --- high search interest with low competition. Save as a Word report with executive summary to the output folder.*

**What Cowork Does**

-   Opens web.

-   Runs searches.

-   Pulls video data.

-   Cross-references results.

-   Categorizes hooks (curiosity gap, shock stat, before-after).

-   Identifies content gaps with reasoning.

-   Writes executive summary.

Done in 12 minutes.

**The Add-On That Adds Personalization**

> *Also, suggest which of these three gaps best fits my channel based on my about-me.md file.*

Now Cowork cross-references opportunities against the user\'s audience, style, and track record. Goes from search tool to research analyst who already knows your business.

**Why You Need Projects (Not Just Folders)**

The pitfall: even with great setup, every fresh task starts with no continuity. Cowork doesn\'t retain task-level context --- what was decided last run, what was adjusted, what good looked like. So users keep re-running similar tasks from scratch.

**Project = Setup + Memory + History**

A project bundles: instructions, context files, work history. Each run builds on prior runs.

**Demonstrated**

-   Create new project: \'Client Deliverables\'.

-   Point at client folder.

-   Add instructions: brand voice, email word limit, Excel column order.

-   Run same six-transcript task --- outputs match column order, tone, formatting automatically.

-   On next run, project contains previous outputs and adjustments.

-   Builds on top of prior work, not from scratch.

**Recommended Project Structure**

-   One project per recurring workflow.

-   Examples: Client Deliverables, Content Production, Admin & Finance, Weekly Reporting.

-   Each becomes more useful with time.

> *At that point, this stops being a tool. It becomes infrastructure.*

**Skills --- Repeatability Layer**

Projects give memory. Skills give repeatability.

**Built-In Skills**

-   Excel --- proper formulas, charts, formatting.

-   PowerPoint --- branded slides.

-   Word --- professional documents.

-   PDF --- generation and modification.

**Custom Skills**

Where Cowork gets dangerously good. Demonstrated example: a content brief skill that produces structure matching the host\'s audience, tone, research depth --- vs generic AI output without it.

Build skills for: weekly reports, sales proposals, SOPs, onboarding documents. Write the playbook once; Claude follows it every time.

**Scheduled Tasks --- The Real Unlock**

Click schedule in sidebar → New task → write prompt → pick frequency → save.

**Host\'s Scheduled Tasks**

**Daily 9 AM --- Morning Briefing**

> *Check Gmail for unread messages from the last 12 hours, check calendar for today\'s events, extract action items and deadlines, save as morning-brief.md in daily-briefings folder.*

**Mondays 12 AM --- Content Research**

> *Search trending topics in AI productivity from the past 7 days, identify three content opportunities based on search volume and engagement patterns, save research brief to content project, post summary to Slack.*

**Three Caveats**

-   Tasks only run while your computer is awake and Claude Desktop is open.

-   Each task counts against usage limits --- keep prompts tight.

-   Always test manually before scheduling.

**Dispatch --- Trigger From Your Phone**

Open Cowork on phone → Enable Dispatch. Setup in 2 minutes.

Use cases the host triggers from outside the office:

-   Content audits from coffee shops.

-   Client deliverables from the car before meetings.

Phone is the remote control. Desktop does the heavy lifting. Auto-routing: code tasks go to Code; knowledge work goes to Cowork.
