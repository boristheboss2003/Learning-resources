**Advanced Prompting (4.1\'s Strengths)**

**Use Structure**

XML-style tags help Claude separate instructions from material. \'Summarize the following article and give 3 takeaways. \<text\>article here\</text\>\' Better focus, prevents mix-ups.

**Assign Roles**

\'You are an experienced hiring manager. Evaluate this resume for a mid-level data analyst role.\' 4.1 handles personas with more nuance than 3.

**Tell It How to Think**

\'Think through the problem step by step. Explain reasoning, then give final answer.\' Paired with extended thinking, see logic before conclusion. Step in mid-process if it makes wrong assumption.

**Show Examples**

Paste a sample, then say \'Now create a new version in this style with the following data.\' 4.1\'s larger context absorbs examples without losing focus.

**Ask Claude to Refine Your Prompt**

\'Rewrite this prompt to be clearer and more detailed.\' Built-in prompt coach.

**Balance Clarity and Creativity**

Too many rules → rigid. Too vague → fluff. Sweet spot: clear instructions + creative freedom. \'Write a 500-word blog post about AI and education in a humorous tone with one anecdote to make it engaging.\'

**Projects --- Persistent Workspaces**

**Extended Thinking (Detailed Tour)**

Toggle below prompt bar. Claude narrates thought process step by step. Sketches pseudo-code, runs calculations, lists options before picking the best. Sample: \'Demand peaks at 5 PM with 460 rides vs 4 AM low. Build strategies around that.\'

**Trade-Off**

-   30 seconds to a minute slower.

-   Output in raw notes form (collapsible if you don\'t want to see).

-   Reading the process is educational --- better prompting yourself.

**When to Use**

-   Debugging code.

-   Analyzing complex datasets.

-   Writing a nuanced essay.

-   NOT \'what\'s the capital of France\' --- chainsaw to cut butter.

**Built-In Data Analysis Tool**

Drag-and-drop a CSV. Ask in plain English. Behind the scenes Claude writes Python, loads CSV, groups, runs math, plots with Matplotlib. Side panel shows interactive charts. Claude interprets the visuals --- turning numbers into stories.

**Pair-Programming Energy**

Paste Python invoice script. \'Review and run this\' --- executes on dummy data. Bug? Claude flags error, explains cause, edits code, shows successful output. Fix not ideal? Nudge alternative --- swap library, refactor function. Adapts smoothly.

**Writing Workflow**

Same rhythm. Have Claude draft. Then surgical edits: \'Make second paragraph funnier and less formal\' --- only that paragraph changes. \'Touch of humor across the whole email and casual sign-off\' --- sprinkles tone without rewriting core content. Huge context window means rapid-fire refinements without losing the thread.

**Parallel Thinking**

\'Summarize this report and check if any numbers contradict the spreadsheet I uploaded.\' Claude analyzes both sources, merges results, returns tight summary plus flagged discrepancies. No second prompt needed.
