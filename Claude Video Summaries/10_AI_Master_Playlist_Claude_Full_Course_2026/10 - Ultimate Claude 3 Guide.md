**Tip 1 --- XML Tags for Format**

Wrap content in pseudo-tags so Claude knows what\'s instructions vs material. Trained specifically to pay attention to XML structure. Random separator characters won\'t work.

Example: \'Summarize this article and give 3 takeaways. \<text\>article here\</text\>\' --- clean separation.

**Tip 2 --- Format Output Specification**

In one prompt, both ask for the task AND show the format you want.

> *First, summarize the key points from the article in \<text\>\</text\> tags and present main conclusions as a 4-bullet list. Then provide an example of how the bullet points should be structured. \<text\>your long text\</text\>*

Massive prompts may show as uploaded text file (Claude\'s UI behavior --- not a problem).

**Tip 3 --- Be Direct and Concise**

AI is predicting the next word --- guessing all the time. Minimize the guessing. Make prompts detailed with clear instructions. \'Extract the key points from this presentation\' works because the task is specific.

**Tip 4 --- Assign Roles**

\'You are a helpful teacher\' or \'You are a creative writer.\' Adjusts tone, approach, level of detail. Makes responses more relevant and aligned.

**Tip 5 --- Explain How to Approach the Task**

AI doesn\'t truly understand what you want --- it predicts. Its \'chain of thought\' (if you can call it that) differs from human thinking. Workaround: ask Claude to first think through the problem, then provide the answer. Use XML tags for thinking section and answer section. Lets you peek into reasoning and tweak as needed.

**Tip 6 --- Ask Claude to Refine Your Prompts**

Claude crafts better prompts than ChatGPT can in this domain. Output goes into Artifacts as a structured prompt with explanations of why it\'s better. Fantastic way to learn prompt engineering.

**Tip 7 --- Examples**

Provide an example for the desired output. Demonstrated: upload calendar PNG, ask for custom calendar with tasks. Use \<example\> tags. Pasting tasks gets you a plain list. Adding example reference gives you a full-blown app inside Claude --- drag/drop tasks, edit, add new ones. Download code or file.

**Tip 8 --- Allow Uncertainty**

Add at end: \'If you\'re not sure, say so.\' AI doesn\'t naturally admit uncertainty --- adds it would. Real factual answers improve.

**Tip 9 --- Decompose Complex Tasks**

Specify steps separately even within one prompt. Example: \'Follow these steps: 1) write a summary. 2) create a bullet list. 3) rewrite the document.\' Forces sequential approach.

**Tip 10 --- Prompt Chaining**

Like assembling furniture --- follow steps in order, each builds on the last. Claude tracks context. Prompts split: analyze → bullet points → summary. With bigger tasks, chaining produces dramatically better results than one mega-prompt.

**Data Analysis Strength**

Upload file, ask for chart. That\'s all. Claude creates readable, useful charts and provides highlights about data and trends. Charts are interactive, not just images. Beats ChatGPT for graphs.
