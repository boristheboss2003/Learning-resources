**Premise**

> *Most people open Claude, see three different models, and get paralyzed. They don\'t know whether to use Haiku, Sonnet, or Opus. They miss powerful features hiding in the interface.*

**Account Setup**

-   claude.ai → create account.

-   Free tier: Sonnet, web search, memory, file creation.

-   Free tier: \~30 messages every 8 hours with Sonnet 4.6.

-   Pro (\$20/mo): 5x usage, Opus 4.6, Cowork access.

**Interface Tour**

-   Top: model selector (defaults Sonnet 4.6).

-   Below: extended thinking toggle (leave off initially).

-   Left sidebar: chat history and projects.

-   Profile → Settings → Custom Instructions (sets context once, applies always).

**Custom Instructions Example**

> *I run a YouTube channel about AI. Assume I understand technical concepts but appreciate practical examples. Focus on real-world applications over theory.*

**The Three Models**

  ------------ --------------------------------------------------------------------- ----------------------
  **Model**    **Use For**                                                           **Cost vs Opus**
  Haiku 4.5    Reformatting, basic summaries, quick questions, bulk processing       Cheapest
  Sonnet 4.6   Daily driver --- writing, coding, analysis, research (90% of tasks)   5x cheaper than Opus
  Opus 4.6     Deep reasoning, complex architecture, gnarly debugging, agent teams   Most expensive
  ------------ --------------------------------------------------------------------- ----------------------

**Decision Framework**

-   Start every task with Sonnet 4.6.

-   Switch to Opus only when Sonnet\'s depth isn\'t enough.

-   Drop to Haiku for bulk processing or quick reformats.

-   Don\'t burn Opus on simple emails.

**Multi-Tab Trick**

Middle-click \'New Chat\' to open in new tab. Run different conversations in parallel without losing context. Game-changer for comparing approaches.

**Extended Thinking --- The Underused Feature**

Toggle below model selector. Claude shows reasoning step-by-step before answering.

**When to Use**

-   Complex analysis where nuance matters.

-   Multi-step problems with dependencies.

-   Anything where \'I need to really think about this\' applies.

-   Decisions with real consequences (pricing, business model).

-   Multivariable problems (5 marketing channels, prioritize 2).

**Real Comparison**

Same prompt, off vs on: \'What pricing model for my online course --- one-time or subscription?\'

-   Off: 5 seconds, generic pros/cons applicable to anyone.

-   On: thinking section visibly considers AI education context, beginner audience, freelancers, churn risk for subscriptions, psychology of beginner buyers, weighing one-time vs recurring for solo creator. Final answer specific and actionable.

**Cost**

-   Uses more message limits.

-   Takes longer.

-   Rule: leave off for \'reformat this list,\' on for decisions that matter.

-   \~20% of host\'s queries use it.

**Memory (Available Even on Free Tier)**

Settings → Capabilities → toggle \'Generate memory from chat history.\' Claude remembers across all conversations, not just within projects.

**First Week Workflow**

1.  Conversation 1: explain work context.

2.  Conversation 2: explain communication preferences.

3.  Conversation 3: explain projects and goals.

After three intentional conversations, Claude has a foundation. Everything builds on top.

**Demo**

Fresh chat: \'Remember these things --- I run AI education for complete beginners. Students are non-technical, no jargon ever. I publish on YouTube and sell online courses. Tone: direct and practical, not academic.\'

Brand-new conversation later: \'Write me an intro for a YouTube lesson explaining what an AI model actually is.\' → Output written for beginners, no jargon, direct tone, structured for YouTube. Memory carried it over automatically.

**Million-Token Context Window (Underrated 2026 Upgrade)**

-   Both Opus and Sonnet 4.6 support 1 million token context.

-   \~750,000 words. Entire book series. Year of emails. Complete codebase.

-   76% accuracy at far edges of million-token context (vs 18% in older models).

**Real Demo**

Upload 10 quarterly reports (80 pages each). Prompt: \'Compare revenue trends across all reports and identify seasonal patterns.\' Claude reads all 800 pages, maintains perfect context, finds patterns missed by host.

**Web Search & Deep Research**

-   Free tier: web search returns information with citations (clickable sources).

-   Pro: Deep Research mode. Claude runs multiple queries, follows threads, synthesizes dozens of sources into comprehensive reports.

**Pro Tip**

Be specific. Not \'research AI trends\' --- \'research how retail businesses under 50 employees are using AI for inventory management, focusing on cost savings and implementation challenges.\'

**Projects**

Persistent workspaces with their own context, knowledge, conversation history.

**Three Things to Always Have in Project Knowledge**

4.  One-page brief --- what this project is, who it\'s for, what success looks like.

5.  Examples of good work --- 3-4 pieces you\'re proud of (Claude calibrates tone/quality).

6.  Constraints and rules --- for YouTube project: \'Always write for beginners, never use jargon without explaining, always end with clear CTA.\'

3 documents, \~1 hour to create. Every conversation in that project dramatically better forever.

**Artifacts**

Right panel: actual structured spreadsheet, not text. Click any cell, ask Claude to change Thursday\'s topic --- only that cell updates. Download as real Excel file.
