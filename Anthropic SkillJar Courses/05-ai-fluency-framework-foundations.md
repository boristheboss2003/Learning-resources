## Overview

A rigorous, academically grounded course on what it means to collaborate with AI effectively, ethically, and safely. Developed in partnership between Anthropic and two university professors (Rick Dakan, Ringling College; Joseph Feller, University College Cork) who created the AI Fluency Framework in 2023–2024 based on research into how AI tools transform creative and business processes.

The core deliverable is the **4D Framework**: four interconnected competencies for effective human-AI collaboration. This framework is foundational for all the AI Fluency course variants (educators, students, nonprofits, teaching).

**Target audience:** Anyone who works with AI and wants a principled, transferable framework — not just tips and tricks. Particularly relevant for team leads, educators, policy people, or anyone thinking seriously about AI adoption.  
**Prerequisites:** None  
**Cost:** Free  
**Instructors:** Drew Bent (Anthropic), Rick Dakan (Ringling College), Joseph Feller (University College Cork), Maggie Vo (Anthropic)

## The 4D Framework — Core Content

This is the intellectual heart of the course and all AI Fluency variants.

### The Four Ds

#### 1. Delegation
*Knowing what to hand to AI vs. what to keep*

- Which tasks are appropriate for AI collaboration and which aren't
- Understanding the difference between augmentation (AI enhances your work) and replacement (AI does work you should be doing yourself)
- Recognising when AI involvement compromises the quality or integrity of the output
- Decision criteria for delegation: what does AI do well, what does it do poorly, what are the stakes of error?

**Operational question: "Should I be doing this with AI at all?"**

#### 2. Description
*Communicating what you want clearly and completely*

- How to write prompts that produce useful outputs (not just "good prompts" but principled prompt construction)
- Providing context, constraints, examples, and format specifications
- The role of iteration — description is a dialogue, not a one-shot command
- Understanding that poor outputs often reflect poor description, not AI failure

**Operational question: "Have I given AI enough to do this well?"**

#### 3. Discernment
*Evaluating and critically assessing AI outputs*

- AI outputs are not ground truth — they must be evaluated
- Recognising hallucination, bias, inappropriate confidence, and gaps
- Domain expertise as the lens for evaluation: you must know enough to judge
- When to trust, when to verify, and when to reject
- The danger of "automation bias" — trusting AI outputs without scrutiny

**Operational question: "Is this output actually correct and appropriate?"**

#### 4. Diligence
*Using AI responsibly, ethically, and safely*

- Disclosure and attribution — when does AI assistance need to be disclosed?
- Privacy and data: what should and shouldn't go into an AI system
- Security risks: prompt injection, data leakage, model behaviour in adversarial contexts
- Systemic effects: what happens when everyone uses AI for this task?
- Organisational and societal responsibility beyond personal use

**Operational question: "Am I using AI in a way I'd be comfortable explaining to others?"**

## How to Operationalise in Our Workflow

### The 4D Framework as a decision checklist
Before any significant AI-assisted task, ask:

1. **Delegation check:** Should I be doing this with AI? Is the task appropriate (stakes, quality requirements, my ability to evaluate the output)?
2. **Description check:** Have I given Claude enough context? Role, task, constraints, format, examples?
3. **Discernment check:** Have I critically evaluated the output? Did I check facts, logic, and appropriateness with my domain expertise?
4. **Diligence check:** Is this use ethical? Does it need disclosure? Are there privacy or security concerns with the data I'm sharing?

Make this a literal checklist for high-stakes work.

### Implementing Discernment practices
- **For any factual claims in AI outputs:** Verify with primary sources before using
- **For any code:** Test it before deploying; understand what it does before accepting
- **For any analysis:** Ask "what would change this conclusion?" and check those things
- **For any creative work:** Ensure it reflects your actual voice and intent, not just a plausible generic version

### Implementing Diligence practices
- **Privacy rule:** Never paste client data, personal information, or confidential documents into Claude without checking if your organisation's terms permit this
- **Disclosure rule:** If you'd be embarrassed to admit AI helped produce something, either don't use AI for it or get comfortable being transparent
- **Security rule:** Don't paste API keys, passwords, or credentials into Claude contexts

### Team/organisational implementation
- Run this course as a team — the 4D Framework creates a shared vocabulary for discussing AI use
- Use Delegation and Diligence as the basis for an AI use policy
- Use Discernment training to address the most common failure mode (uncritical acceptance of AI output)

### Why this course is underrated
Most AI training focuses on tool mechanics. This course builds the judgment layer that makes tool use actually reliable. The 4D Framework is tool-agnostic — it works regardless of which model or product you're using. This makes it the most durable investment in AI skill-building.
