## Overview

The entry-point course for anyone beginning to use Claude in their daily work. This is a non-technical, practical orientation — not about building with the API or coding, but about becoming a confident Claude user for everyday knowledge work. Covers the full Claude product surface: Chat, Cowork, and Code at a high level, plus Projects, Artifacts, Skills, integrations, and Research Mode.

**Target audience:** Knowledge workers, business users, anyone new to Claude  
**Prerequisites:** None

## Full Curriculum

### Section 1 — Meet Claude
- **What is Claude?** — Introduction to Claude as an AI assistant; likely covers Anthropic's mission, Claude's character, and the difference from search or simple chatbots
- **Your first conversation with Claude** — Hands-on: starting a conversation, understanding the interface, basic message patterns
- **Getting better results** — Core prompting literacy: how to be specific, give context, iterate; likely introduces the idea that Claude is a collaborator not just a query engine
- **Claude desktop app: Chat, Cowork, Code** — Overview of the three modes of the desktop app. Chat = conversation; Cowork = agentic file/folder tasks; Code = Claude Code for developers

### Section 2 — Organizing Your Work and Knowledge
- **Introduction to projects** — Projects are persistent workspaces where you can group conversations, files, and context. Key for managing ongoing work rather than one-off chats
- **Creating with artifacts** — Artifacts are standalone outputs (documents, code, SVGs, etc.) that Claude produces and you can iterate on; distinct from in-conversation messages
- **Working with skills** — Skills (in the Claude app context) are reusable instruction sets that shape how Claude behaves in specific contexts; this is the end-user version of what Claude Code calls Skills

### Section 3 — Expanding Claude's Reach
- **Connecting your tools** — Integrations/MCP connections: linking Claude to external services (Google Drive, Notion, etc.)
- **Enterprise search** — Claude's ability to search across connected organizational data sources; relevant for teams with enterprise Anthropic plans
- **Research mode for deep dives** — Research Mode enables multi-step web research with citations; Claude autonomously searches, synthesizes, and reports

### Section 4 — Putting It All Together

## How to Operationalise in Our Workflow

### Immediate actions
1. **Register and complete this course first** if you haven't used Claude systematically before. It maps the full product surface in ~1 hour.
2. **Set up Projects immediately after.** Every ongoing piece of work (client, project, research area) should have its own Project with relevant files pinned. Stop using ad-hoc chats for anything recurring.
3. **Learn the three modes explicitly.** When you sit down to work with Claude, ask: is this a Chat task (conversation/thinking), Cowork task (file operations on my machine), or Code task (developer workflow)?

### Prompting upgrades from this course
- After completing "Getting better results": Audit your last 10 Claude conversations. Where were you vague? Rewrite those prompts with role, context, format, and constraints explicitly stated.
- After "Introduction to projects": Create one Project per active work area. Add your most-referenced documents to each.
- After "Working with skills": Identify your top 3 recurring Claude tasks. Write a Skill for each.

### Workflow integration points
- **Research Mode** → Use for any question that requires more than one source. Don't just chat; switch to Research Mode for literature reviews, competitive research, policy lookups.
- **Artifacts** → Any output you want to edit, share, or reuse should be produced as an Artifact. Train yourself to say "produce this as an artifact" when you want a document.
- **Connecting your tools** → Prioritise connecting Google Drive and your note-taking tool first. This gives Claude ambient context about your actual work.

### For our specific context
- This course is foundational for onboarding anyone new to Claude in our setup
- The "use-cases by role" section is directly relevant for demonstrating Claude's value to stakeholders
- Skills covered here overlap with the Cowork and Agent Skills courses — treat this as the map, those as the deep dives
