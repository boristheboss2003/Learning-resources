## Overview

An applied version of the AI Fluency Framework specifically for faculty, instructional designers, and educational leaders. Builds directly on "AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations" but focuses the 4D Framework on actual teaching practice: course design, learning materials, assessment, and modelling responsible AI collaboration for students.

Created in partnership with professors Rick Dakan (Ringling College) and Joseph Feller (University College Cork) — the same educators who developed the original AI Fluency Framework. Supported by Ireland's Higher Education Authority.

**Target audience:** Academic faculty, instructional designers, curriculum developers, educational administrators  
**Prerequisites:** "AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations" recommended (this course builds on it)  
**Cost:** Registration required  
**Format:** Section-based course

## Full Curriculum

### Section 1 — Introduction and AI Fluency Framework
- Re-introduction to the 4D Framework in an educational context
- How Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence apply differently when you're an educator vs. a general knowledge worker
- The dual role: educator as AI user AND educator as AI-literacy teacher
- Framing question: how can I apply this framework to my personal teaching practice?

### Section 2 — AI Fluency Framework Applications for Educators
The core applied section. Likely covers:

**Using AI as a thinking partner in course design:**
- Using Claude to brainstorm learning objectives
- Generating draft syllabi and lesson outlines
- Identifying gaps in curriculum coverage
- Adapting content for different learning levels
- How to critically evaluate and modify AI-generated course materials (Discernment applied to education)

**Creating coherent learning materials:**
- Using AI to generate first drafts of readings, exercises, case studies
- Consistency checking across a course's materials
- Adapting existing materials for new contexts
- Multi-modal content generation (lecture notes → slides → discussion questions → assessments)

**Developing authentic assessments:**
- The challenge: if AI can do the assignment, what does the assessment actually measure?
- Designing AI-resistant assessments (process-based, contextual, oral)
- Designing AI-integrated assessments (using AI as part of the task)
- Assessment criteria for AI-assisted work

**Modelling responsible AI collaboration:**
- How educators model the 4D Framework in their own teaching
- Transparency about AI use in the classroom
- Teaching students to be the "human in the loop"
- Institutional policy navigation

| Concept | What it is | Why it matters for educators |
|---|---|---|
| AI as thinking partner | Using Claude to enhance course design process | Reduces prep time; surfaces blind spots |
| Authentic assessment | Tasks AI can't simply complete for students | Ensures assessment still measures learning |
| AI-integrated assessment | AI as part of the task, not a cheat | Prepares students for real-world AI use |
| Modelling | Educator demonstrating 4D Framework use | Students learn by watching, not just reading |
| Institutional policy | Navigating org rules about AI use | Educators work within constraints |

## The 4D Framework Applied to Teaching

| D | Teaching application |
|---|---|
| **Delegation** | What parts of course design are appropriate to delegate to AI? (Brainstorming ✓, Final assessment design ✗) |
| **Description** | How to prompt Claude effectively for educational outputs; providing context about students, learning level, discipline |
| **Discernment** | Critically evaluating AI-generated syllabi, materials, and assessments with domain expertise |
| **Diligence** | Disclosure to students; copyright of AI-generated content; data privacy in educational contexts |

## How to Operationalise in Our Workflow

*(Adapted for professional/organisational learning contexts, not just academia)*

### If you're designing training or learning programmes
The educators' framework applies directly to professional L&D:

1. **Course design with AI as thinking partner**
   - Use Claude to draft learning objectives → then critically review (Discernment)
   - Generate first-draft workshop materials → adapt for your audience and organisation
   - Produce discussion questions, case studies, scenarios from your actual work context

2. **Assessment design for professional learning**
   - Avoid pure knowledge-recall assessments (AI can answer these)
   - Design assessments around: applying judgment, navigating ambiguity, explaining reasoning
   - Consider process-based assessment: "show how you used AI to solve this problem" vs. "produce the solution"

3. **Modelling for your team/organisation**
   - Be explicit about when and how you use AI in your work
   - Demonstrate the 4D checklist in practice, not just in training
   - Create a culture where AI use is discussed openly, not hidden or stigmatised

### For onboarding and team training
- This course's approach to "modelling AI collaboration" is exactly what team leads should do: show your process, not just your outputs
- The "authentic assessment" principles apply to evaluating whether team members have actually developed AI fluency vs. just learned to use Claude mechanically

### Immediate action
1. Complete "AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations" first
2. Then use this course to design a team AI fluency programme
3. The key output: a 4D-informed training session your team can go through, with assessments that test actual judgment, not tool knowledge
