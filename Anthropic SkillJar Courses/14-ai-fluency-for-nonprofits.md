## Why a Nonprofit-Specific Course?

Nonprofits face unique AI considerations that general AI Fluency courses don't address:
1. **Limited resources** — Small teams with minimal tech support; can't afford extensive experimentation or failures
2. **Multiple stakeholder accountabilities** — Board, donors, beneficiaries, regulators, public — each with different expectations about AI use
3. **Mission integrity** — AI use must align with organisational values and not undermine the mission or trust of constituents
4. **Data sensitivity** — Client/beneficiary data is often sensitive; privacy and consent are paramount
5. **Digital equity** — Nonprofits often serve populations with less digital access; AI tools shouldn't widen that gap

## Full Curriculum

### Section 1 — Introduction
- The 4D Framework in a nonprofit context
- How nonprofits' constraints and values shape appropriate AI use
- From "should we use AI?" to "how do we use AI responsibly?"
- Overview of AI landscape for nonprofits: what's available, what's realistic

### Section 2 — Description-Discernment
Two Ds in sequence — the practical communication and evaluation pair:

**Description in nonprofit contexts:**
- Prompting for mission-aligned outputs: how to communicate organisational values, voice, and constraints to Claude
- Fundraising and communications use cases: grant writing, donor communications, campaign copy, impact reports
- Programme design: using Claude to think through theory of change, programme logic, evaluation criteria
- Operations: standard operating procedures, HR communications, board materials
- Providing Claude with the context it needs about your organisation's history, beneficiaries, and values

**Discernment in nonprofit contexts:**
- Evaluating AI outputs through a mission lens: does this reflect our values?
- Fact-checking: nonprofits are scrutinised; AI hallucinations about statistics or claims are reputationally dangerous
- Voice and authenticity: does this sound like us? Does it authentically represent our beneficiaries?
- Bias in outputs: AI systems can replicate biases that harm the communities nonprofits serve
- When to reject AI assistance: high-stakes communications, sensitive beneficiary situations, anything requiring lived experience

### Section 3 — Delegation-Diligence
The strategic and ethical pair:

**Delegation in nonprofit contexts:**
- What to delegate: administrative tasks, first drafts, research, data analysis
- What not to delegate: decisions affecting beneficiaries, communications requiring authentic voice, sensitive case management
- Resource allocation logic: where does AI give the most leverage for limited staff time?
- Volunteer and board considerations: how AI affects roles and capacity

**Diligence in nonprofit contexts:**
- **Data privacy for beneficiaries** — Never input beneficiary PII into AI systems without consent frameworks. Treat client data as strictly confidential.
- **Disclosure to stakeholders** — When should donors, board members, or beneficiaries know AI was used?
- **Grant application ethics** — Many funders have emerging policies on AI use in applications; understand and comply
- **Equity implications** — How does your AI use affect digital equity? Does it make your organisation more or less accessible?
- **Organisational AI policy** — Developing an internal policy that reflects nonprofit values

### Section 4 — Putting It All Together
- Integrated case studies from nonprofit contexts (likely: grant writing, programme design, donor communications, annual report)
- Building an AI use culture in a nonprofit: inclusive, mission-driven, transparent
- Implementation pathway: where to start, how to expand responsibly
- Resources and next steps

## Nonprofit-Specific Privacy Checklist

Before using Claude with any organisational data:
- [ ] Does this data contain beneficiary PII? (If yes: stop — requires special handling)
- [ ] Does this data contain donor PII beyond what's needed? (Minimise)
- [ ] Is this a staff or volunteer personal matter? (High sensitivity)
- [ ] Would we be comfortable if the data appeared in our Claude conversation history? (Governance check)
- [ ] Does our donor/grant agreement prohibit sharing this data with third parties? (Legal check)

## How to Operationalise in Our Workflow

*(Reframed for mission-driven organisations and teams with accountability to external stakeholders)*

### If you work with or advise nonprofits
This course provides the framework for:
- Consulting on responsible AI adoption for mission-driven organisations
- Designing AI training programmes for nonprofit teams
- Developing AI governance policies appropriate to nonprofit constraints

### AI policy template (from course principles)
A minimal nonprofit AI policy should cover:
1. **Scope** — Which tools are approved; which data can be used in which tools
2. **Beneficiary protection** — Absolute prohibition on inputting beneficiary PII
3. **Disclosure** — When to disclose AI use to donors, funders, board
4. **Quality review** — All AI outputs reviewed by a qualified human before external use
5. **Training** — All staff to complete basic AI Fluency training before using AI tools
6. **Review** — Policy reviewed every 6 months as the landscape evolves

### Grant writing guidance
Many funders are now requiring or asking about AI use:
- Check each funder's AI policy before submitting
- Some funders prohibit AI; others require disclosure; others are neutral
- If disclosing: frame as "AI assisted in drafting; all content reviewed and approved by [person with domain expertise]"
- Never claim AI-written content is purely human-authored

### For our specific context
- Even if we're not a nonprofit, the mission-alignment and stakeholder-accountability lens is useful for any organisation with external accountability
- The equity and disclosure principles are best-practice for any organisation, not just nonprofits
- The limited-resource leverage framework is useful for any team deciding where to invest AI adoption effort
